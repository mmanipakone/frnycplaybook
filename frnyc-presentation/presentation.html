Section 01 — Recruitment Architecture

The Five-Stage Pipeline

These five stages are sequential. Skipping or compressing any stage reduces conversion at the next. Awareness without exploration is just information. Exploration without commitment is just curiosity.

Stage 01

Awareness

Students learn FRNYC exists for people like them.

⚡ Curiosity is fragile — decays within days without follow-up

Stage 02

Exploration

Meaningful contact: panels, site visits, near-peer presentations.

Goal: identity integration — "this is for someone like me"

Stage 03

Commitment

Decision Day as a formal ritual. Public commitments are more durable than private ones.

⚡ Summer gap erodes commitment — check in

Stage 04

Experience

Relevant curriculum, strong teachers, WBL placements matched to interests.

Stage 05

Completion

Credential earned. Completers are next year's strongest recruiters.

"Stickiness comes from coupling awareness with exposure in a structured sequence. At a point when interest is integrated into students' sense of self, identity is more durable than interest."

Section 02 — 8th Grade Recruitment

Open House Strategy & NYCDOE App Signal

Key Moment

The open house 2–3 weeks before the NYCDOE application deadline is the highest-leverage event in 8th grade recruitment. Everything else supports it.

What to Do at Open Houses

1

Deploy Student Ambassadors

Current FRNYC students speak 2–3 minutes with a clear script. Authenticity converts.

2

Show Real Student Work

Portfolios, credentials, project demos — not program brochures.

3

Walk the App Together

Announce pathway codes and help families complete the NYCDOE app in the room.

The NYCDOE App as Signal of Interest

Pathway Code = First Signal

Students who list an FRNYC school's pathway code as 1st or 2nd choice have self-identified as interested. Track these students. Follow up within 48 hours of the app window closing.

After the Open House

  • Follow up within 48 hours with interested families
  • Share pathway Outcome/Skills/Experience/Payoff brief
  • Students blocked by scheduling get an individual conversation — not a form rejection
⚡

Year 0 tip: 2 high-quality open houses beat 5 generic ones. Depth converts. Breadth builds name recognition only.

The most important recruitment event of the year happens inside your school, in May, with every 9th grader.

8 / 16
Section 03 — Internal Recruitment Cycle

Interest Survey → Review & Reconcile → Confirm

Phase 01 — Sept to Spring Break

Generate Awareness

  • Career Explorations course introduces pathways to 9th graders
  • Near-peer presentations in all 9th grade advisories — seniors including those NOT in FRNYC
  • Schools with Career Explorations preview courses report 75%+ opt-in from 9th graders who attended
Phase 02 — May (Critical)

Interest Survey

  • PowerPoint in all 9th grade advisories first
  • Google Form survey to every 9th grader within 1–2 weeks
  • Critical design: NOT an opt-in. Every student must respond: Yes / No / Maybe
  • Ensures no one is missed and no one is assumed to be uninterested
Key Moment

The opt-in AND opt-out survey to all 9th graders in May is the highest-leverage action of the internal cycle.

Phase 03 — May/June

Review, Reconcile & Confirm

  • Principal + AP of Instruction + grade team review results together
  • Cross-reference against master schedule: IEP services, AP conflicts, grade repetition
  • Students who said yes but can't be scheduled get an individual conversation
  • Confirmed students receive acceptance letter + branded materials
  • Summer gap touchpoint before September — postcard, group chat, or call
⚡

The summer gap is an underused retention touchpoint. The period between June acceptance and September arrival consistently erodes commitment. A postcard or a group chat message costs almost nothing and prevents enthusiasm from fading.

Section 04 — Pathway Information Design

The Outcome / Skills / Experience / Payoff Framework

Every FRNYC pathway must answer these four questions — specifically and concretely. Students who choose pathways aligned to personal meaning stay. Students who were placed or drifted into a pathway often leave.

The Outcome — Why It Matters

What careers does this open?

  • Clear careers and associated wages (specific dollar amounts)
  • Tangible benefits: income, credentials, college credits
  • Avoid: "career readiness," "industry exposure," "21st century skills"
The Skills — What Students Will Learn

What will students be able to do?

  • Specific, practical skills — focus on what students can do
  • Concrete demonstrations: portfolio, certification, project
  • Avoid: abstract learning objectives
The Experience — What the Day Looks Like

What will students actually do?

  • Projects, fieldwork, and WBL examples
  • Real-world exposure: sites, partners, environments
  • Photos of actual student work — not stock images
The Payoff — What They Leave With

What do they have at graduation?

  • Industry-recognized certifications earned
  • College credits completed before graduation
  • Work experience and internship documentation

Section 05 — Equity

SWD & MLL: Barriers and Actionable Fixes

SWD and MLL students are frequently underrepresented in FRNYC pathways — not because they aren't interested, but because structural barriers push them out. Strong programs actively track who is and isn't enrolled, set explicit equity targets, and problem-solve barriers before they become attrition.

1
Scheduling Conflicts (SWD)

IEP mandated services and AP placement pull SWD students from pathway slots before anyone notices.

Fix

Principal + AP cross-reference survey results with master schedule. Students blocked by schedule get individual conversations — not form rejections.

2
Language Access (MLL)

English-only materials mean MLL families receive less information and make less-informed decisions.

Fix

Translate all core pathway materials into top 3 home languages. Recruit bilingual near-peer ambassadors. Partner with ENL department for co-outreach.

3
ECC Prerequisites (Both)

Prerequisites disproportionately exclude SWD and MLL students who are on track but not in accelerated sequences.

Fix

Audit prerequisite lists annually. Identify which barriers are structural requirements vs. inherited assumptions. Work with OSP to expand access pathways.

4
Representation Gap

Materials and ambassador corps with limited SWD/MLL representation signal "this pathway is not for people like me."

Fix

Intentionally recruit SWD and MLL alumni as ambassadors. Review all visual materials for representation before publication.

⚡

Equity target: The % of enrolled FRNYC students who are SWD and MLL should reflect the school's population. Significant gaps trigger a school-level equity review — not a communications fix.

Action Plan

Next Steps by Role & School Year

Year 0 — New FRNYC Schools
  • Designate your FRNYC coordinator before November 1
  • Confirm NYCDOE pathway codes; put them on all open house materials
  • Schedule your first open house 6–8 weeks before app deadline
  • Update all pathway descriptions to Outcome/Skills/Experience/Payoff format
  • Set Year 0 enrollment target with OSP support
  • Attend FRNYC coordinator onboarding sessions
Year 1+ — Existing FRNYC Schools
  • Review last year's funnel data — where did conversion drop?
  • Rebuild ambassador corps from last year's completers by October 15
  • Close identified equity gaps (SWD, MLL) with a specific plan
  • Deploy opt-in AND opt-out interest survey to all 9th graders in May
  • Send summer gap touchpoint to confirmed cohort before July 1
  • Complete post-season debrief by August 31

"Recruitment is not an event; it is a year-round process that begins in 8th grade. Schools that wait until 10th grade to introduce pathways consistently struggle to fill cohorts with genuinely interested students."
